Jerk Chicken Legs

Description

Jerk is a style of cooking native to Jamaica, in which meat is dry-rubbed or wet marinated with a hot spice mixture called Jamaican jerk spice. Historians believe that jerking originated with the Amerindians in Jamaica from the Arawak and Ta√≠no tribes who intermingled with the Maroons. The smoky taste of jerked meat is achieved using various cooking methods, including modern wood-burning ovens. The meat is normally chicken or pork, and the main ingredients of the spicy jerk marinade sauce are allspice and Scotch bonnet peppers. Jerk cooking is popular in Caribbean and West Indian diaspora communities throughout North America and Western Europe.

Ingredients

  • 12 chicken legs or thighs
  • 1 bunch of spring onions
  • 5 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 1" fresh ginger, peeled and sliced
  • 2 habanero peppers, stemmed and roughly chopped
  • 1/2 yellow onion, roughly diced
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup neutral oil, preferably canola
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 2 tsp ground allspice
  • 2 tsp pirate's bite
  • 2 tsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 lime, juice only
  • 1 lemon, juice only

Steps/Instructions

  1. In a blender, combine everything excluding the chicken. Blend until smooth - set aside, reserving 1/2 cup.
  2. Place chicken in marinade, making sure all is covered well. Refigerate at least two hours or up to overnight. Remove from fridge 30 minutes prior to cooking.
  3. Preheat oven to 350*. Prepare "basting" spray by adding beer to a spray bottle.
  4. Add chicken to a foil lined baking dish. Roast for approx. one hour basting every 20 mins, or as needed.
  5. Remove from ovena nd chop into pieces. Serve.
